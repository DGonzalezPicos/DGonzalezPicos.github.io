About

PhD candidate at Leiden Observatory (2023-2027) on high-resolution spectroscopy of exoplanets, brown dwarfs and cool stars. I work with ground-based (VLT/CRIRES+) and space-based instruments (JWST/NIRSpec) to study the composition, thermal structure and general atmospheric properties of hot and cool objects. I focus on the measurement and interpretation of atmospheric isotope ratios and their potential link to formation pathways.

What I Work On

Exoplanet Atmospheres

Investigating the composition, structure, and dynamics of exoplanet atmospheres with a focus on directly-imaged planets and brown dwarfs.

High-Resolution Spectroscopy

Conducting observations and analyzing high-resolution spectra with ground-based instruments and recently medium-resolution spectroscopy from space with JWST/NIRSpec.

Bayesian Retrievals

Applying Bayesian inference techniques to extract key atmospheric parameters from observational data, ensuring robust, probabilistic insights into atmospheric properties.

Data Reduction and Calibration

Developing and refining methods for processing raw astronomical data, ensuring accurate calibration to maximize the scientific output

Isotope Ratios

Studying isotopic compositions in atmospheres of planets, brown dwarfs and cool stars to reveal insights about formation histories, chemical processes, and environmental conditions of distant planets and stars.

Open-Source Astronomy Software

Creating and contributing to open-source software tools that support the astronomical community, enabling more accessible data analysis, visualization, and collaboration.
